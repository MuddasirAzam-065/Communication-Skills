WHAT IS PROPOSAL WRITING?

A proposal is a formal written document that presents an idea, solution, or plan to persuade a reader ,decision-maker, stakeholder, or client  to approve, fund, or act upon it.

![Checkbox Checked with solid fill](Graphic24.jpg)

![Presentation with org chart outline](Graphic28.jpg)

![Question Mark with solid fill](Graphic22.jpg)
Identify a Problem
Propose a Solution
Persuade & Justify
Clearly states an existing challenge or need that demands attention
Offers a concrete, well reasoned answer to address the problem
Uses evidence, cost benefit analysis and logic to earn approval

DOS AND DON'TS IN PROPOSAL WRITING

DO

DON'T

Research the problem thoroughly before writing
Use vague language like 'we will try' or 'maybe'

Use clear, plain language avoid jargon
Exaggerate benefits or underestimate costs

Tailor the proposal to your specific audience
Write for yourself always write for the reader

Support every claim with data or evidence
Submit without proofreading for errors

Keep it concise quality over quantity
Include irrelevant information to bulk up the document

Use headings and visuals for easy scanning
Use a template without customising it

Write the executive summary last
Forget to include a clear call to action

Proofread multiple times before submitting
Send without getting a peer review first

KEY TAKEAWAYS

A proposal is a persuasive document always write for your reader, not for yourself

Internal proposals are the most common type in professional life master this first

Start with a clear, specific problem vague problems produce vague proposals

Every claim needs evidence data, statistics, and cost-benefit analysis are essential

Follow the structure: Title → Executive Summary → Problem → Solution → Plan → Budget → CTA

The Executive Summary, Budget and Conclusion are the three most-read sections perfect them

Always proofread, get feedback, and tailor your proposal before submission
