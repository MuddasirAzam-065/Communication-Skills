WHAT IS PROPOSAL WRITING?

Definition:
A proposal is a formal document to persuade a decision-maker to approve, fund, or act.

3 Core Parts:
• Identify a Problem
• Propose a Solution
• Persuade & Justify (evidence + ROI)

BUSINESS PROPOSALS: INTERNAL vs EXTERNAL

INTERNAL (within organisation)
• Writer: employee/department
• Reader: management
• Goal: approval for resources/change
• Tone: professional + familiar

EXTERNAL (outside organisation)
• Writer: sales/company
• Reader: client/partner
• Goal: win contract
• Tone: formal + persuasive

DOS AND DON'TS IN PROPOSAL WRITING

DO:
• Research the problem
• Write clearly (no jargon)
• Tailor to audience
• Use evidence
• Keep it concise
• Use headings/visuals
• Write executive summary last
• Proofread + peer review

DON’T:
• Use vague words (“try”, “maybe”)
• Inflate benefits / hide costs
• Write for yourself
• Submit without editing
• Add irrelevant info
• Copy template without customising
• Forget call to action

KEY TAKEAWAYS

• Proposal = persuasive document (reader-focused)
• Internal proposals are most common
• Clear problem → strong proposal
• Evidence + cost-benefit is essential
• Follow structure (Title → Summary → Problem → Solution → Plan → Budget → CTA)
• Most-read: Executive Summary, Budget, Conclusion
• Proofread, get feedback, tailor
